To the California Department of Education; and Instructional Quality Commission:
I'm writing to you on behalf of Club Z, a national Zionist youth movement headquartered in California, and with the support of over 4,000 individuals who signed our petition (enclosed). We would like to thank you and other California officials for acknowledging that the current draft of the ethnic studies curriculum "needs to be substantially redesigned," and for taking the first step in rectifying this problematic curriculum.
The signees of our petition come from diverse backgrounds, yet they are all united in one message: that there is no place for hate and discrimination in California's public schools, and that there should be zerotolerance for antisemitism in any form.
As the Executive Director of Club Z, I am reaching out ask about the measures that will be taken to ensure that those who are tasked to draft the new curriculum will not insert their personal bias into an academic document that will affect public school student across California. Our Zionist youth movement teaches pride in the Jewish and Zionist experience. For your stated goal of ethnic studies to serve as "an important tool to improve school climate and increase our understanding of one another," it is critical that the Jewish and Zionist narrative be represented in the new draft curriculum.
I look forward to hearing from you and to working together to ensure that the new draft of the curriculum promotes inclusivity and tolerance for all.
Respectfully,
Masha Merkulova Founder & Executive Director Club Z
