While I believe it is important for students to engage with history, including the ugly and nasty parts of history, this curriculum in its current form, is not an appropriate way of doing that. There is no critical thinking taught; there are no differing perspectives given; there is no room for discussion or thoughtful wrestling with complex historical issues. Rather, this is an exercise in groupthink--an attempt to get all students to think the same way on an issue. Certainly history, and the moral decisions made throughout it, are more complex than the one-sided version given throughout this curriculum.

Also, the curriculum is filled with a ridiculously complex vocabulary that is of no use to anyone, let alone teenagers. This is the view of one particular group of people, not the view of the majority of Americans, and certainly not one that most parents want their children taught.
