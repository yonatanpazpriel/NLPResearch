David Lewis (self)

Although I fully agree that Muslims and Arabs are often unfairly stereotyped, and it is good that the curriculum makes an attempt to address this issue, there are many other groups that are often stereo typed and discriminated against, but the curriculum seems to place a significantly higher emphasis on discussing Muslim/Arad stereotypes, and does not discuss much African American, Hispanic, and Jewish stereo types. I did not see any mention of antisemitism, for example.

I notices that the curriculum mentions that some of the immigrants from the Ottoman Empire were not Arabs or Muslims, and give the example of Jews, but I did not see anything about the massive numbers of Jewish immigrants from Eastern Europe, before and after World War two, fleeing antisemitism, and abject poverty caused by anti-Semitic policies.

The curriculum appears to be one-sided on Israeli/Palestine issues. Often AntiIsrael is used as a proxy for anti-Jewish, and this curriculum does nothing to counter that. There is no reference to Israel's legitimate defense concerns and of Israel's attempts to negotiate peace. BDS and similar organization are mentioned, which have some significant segments that have anti-Semitic
